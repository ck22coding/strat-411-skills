# Phase 1: Problem Framing — Detailed Steps

> Loaded by SKILL.md at the Phase 1 gate. Follow all steps in order.

---

## Step 1: Read the Case

The user will provide a case document or description. Read and internalize everything.

Before analyzing the case, establish the output convention:
1. Set the **case prefix** — ask: "What short name for file naming? e.g., `airbnb-case`"
2. Set the **working directory** — ask: "Where should working files go? I suggest `~/Desktop/{case-prefix}/`"
3. Create the directory if it doesn't exist.

Read `assets/output-convention.md` for naming rules. Reference `assets/deliverables-table.md` for the full file manifest.

These carry forward through all phases.

Read `references/scqa-framework.md` for the SCQA framing methodology.

Your goal is to frame the case using the SCQA structure — a precise problem definition is the foundation of everything that follows; answering the wrong problem is the most common mistake in case analysis:

**Situation** (Questions 1-3):
1. What is the history of the industry? Consider: levels of competition, shifts in competition, growth patterns, technological shifts.
2. What is the history of the target company?
3. What is the current situation?

**Complication** (Question 4):
4. What is the core problem the firm is facing? What changed, went wrong, or threatens the firm?

**Question** (make explicit):
5. What should the firm do about this? Frame the specific strategic question the case must answer.

## Step 2: Industry & Firm Analysis

Before researching, ask the user: "What time period should I focus on? How far back should I look at this industry and company?"

Then invoke `/411-research` in **standalone mode** with a research brief covering four topics:

- **Industry dynamics** — Is this an industry where leaders are frequently displaced by new entrants, or do the same firms dominate for decades? How easy is it for competitors to copy innovations? Does success in this industry build on itself over time?
- **Competitive landscape** — Who are the key players? What are their relative positions? Are there clear strategic groups? What has changed recently? What is the magnitude of the change? What recent initiatives, investments, or moves signal the firm's current strategy?
- **Firm capabilities** — What is the firm known for? What routines and processes define how it operates? Could those same strengths become constraints?
- **Change signals** — Is the industry undergoing a technology shift, regulatory change, or demand disruption? How fast is the landscape moving?

The Source Ledger from this invocation carries forward into Phase 5 — it becomes the starting point for the combined research record. Use `/browse` for any targeted follow-up questions that arise from Step 3 (Confirm Understanding).

**Apply theory lenses during research.** Read `references/rugged-landscapes.md` and `references/schumpeterian.md` to analyze the industry through multiple strategic lenses: Is competition Schumpeterian or routinized? How rugged is the landscape (how interdependent are the firm's strategic choices)? What routines constrain the firm? Are there signs of creative destruction?

**Platform diagnostic.** If the firm is a platform business facing a growth or scope question (not just competitive positioning), read `references/platform-strategy.md` and additionally map: current sides, current interactions, current engagement levers, and competitor portfolio scope. Present this mapping to the user as part of Step 3 framing.

This research informs your framing — present findings naturally when confirming understanding in Step 3, not as a separate deliverable.

## Step 3: Confirm Understanding

Ask first: "Before I share my read — what stands out to you as the core problem?" Incorporate their view, then present your answers to the four questions, enriched by your industry research.

Ask: "Does this look correct? Anything missing or framed differently than you'd put it?"

**Do NOT proceed to Phase 2 until the user confirms.**
